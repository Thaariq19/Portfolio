Group G18 Project Log

Week 2 — Review of Existing Pipelines

Status: Completed | Dates: 04/05/2026 – 10/05/2026

Goals for the Week

  • Evaluate how current change detection models fuse bi-temporal features.
  • Conceptualize a new temporal feature construction strategy.

Tasks Completed

  • Investigated standard Siamese architectures that rely heavily on absolute differencing (|F2 - F1|).
  • Identified a critical flaw in existing pipelines: absolute difference captures change magnitude but discards temporal direction, making the appearance and disappearance of objects indistinguishable.
  • Proposed a new differential feature construction method that retains paired features, absolute differences, and signed differences to give the network direction-aware evidence.

Technical Challenges

  • Ensuring that preserving signed temporal evidence does not drastically increase the computational overhead of the feature fusion block.

Next Steps

  • Proceed to Week 3: Design the benchmarking framework and the region-boundary separation architecture.
