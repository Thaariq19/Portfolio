Week 1 — Literature Review and Problem Definition

Status: Completed | Dates: 20/04/2026 – 26/04/2026

Goals for the Week

  • Conduct a literature review on remote sensing change detection, evaluating CNNs, Transformers, and Visual State-Space Models (SSMs).
  • Define the core localization challenges in binary change detection.

Tasks Completed

  • Analyzed existing CNN encoder-decoder models and noted that their context modeling is bounded by the receptive field.
  • Evaluated Transformer and Mamba-based methods, determining that while they offer stronger global representations, they do not automatically resolve boundary localization errors.
  • Formulated the primary problem definition: encoder scaling alone is insufficient for boundary-sensitive tasks; the network must explicitly preserve fine boundaries.

Technical Challenges

  • Identifying why models detect changed regions accurately but still produce shifted or over-smoothed masks near object boundaries.

Visual Evidence/Code

Literature review compiled, highlighting boundary delineation as the dominant failure mode under domain shift.
