Group G18 Project Log

Week 1 — Literature Review and Problem Definition

Status: Completed | Dates: 27/04/2026 – 03/05/2026

Goals for the Week

  • Conduct a foundational literature review moving from Transformer-based to State-Space Model (SSM) architectures for change detection.
  • Define the computational and boundary-localization problems in current models.

Tasks Completed

  • Analyzed the limitations of standard self-attention in dense prediction tasks based on the original Transformer architecture.
  • Reviewed ChangeFormer to understand how Siamese Transformers currently handle bi-temporal feature fusion.
  • Investigated the foundational Mamba paper to evaluate linear-time sequence modeling as a computationally efficient alternative to Transformers.

Technical Challenges

  • Understanding the mathematical transition from standard attention mechanisms to selective state-space sequence modeling.

Next Steps

  • Proceed to Week 2: Review existing pipelines and adapt state-space models for 2D visual representations.

References

  • A. Vaswani et al., "Attention is all you need," in Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2017.
  • W. G. C. Bandara and V. M. Patel, "A transformer-based siamese network for change detection," in IEEE International Geoscience and Remote Sensing Symposium (IGARSS), 2022, pp. 207-210.
  • A. Gu and T. Dao, "Mamba: Linear-time sequence modeling with selective state spaces," arXiv preprint arXiv:2312.00752, 2023.
